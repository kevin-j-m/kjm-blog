## Blue Ridge Ruby 2026

I returned to Asheville for the first time since [2023]({{< ref "blue-ridge-ruby-2023-recap" >}}). Both times for the Blue Ridge Ruby conference. Both times convinced for the same reason: organizer Jeremy Smith. Both times because I was selected as a speaker.

This post is not intended as a review of any of the talks, but to highlight the variety of great work from all involved. I hope you’ll seek out the full videos of all the sessions that interest you once they are available.

## Preparing

Jeremy Smith conducted interviews with many of the speakers prior to the conference. Jeremy and I [chatted]({{< ref "blue-ridge-notes" >}}) about various topics related to conferences and writing. Beyond that, I mostly followed [my own advice]({{< ref "building-conference-talk-content" >}}).

## Day 1

### Learning from Permaculture: Sustainable Software Development

[John Athayde](https://blueridgeruby.com/speakers/john-athayde/) shared his
knowledge of permaculture and how to apply that to our work. I particularly
enjoyed the perspective to allow for a growing season to appreciate and
understand what already exists before making sweeping changes.

{{< figure src="/img/blue_ridge_2026_john.jpg" class="mid" alt="John at Blue Ridge Ruby 2026" >}}

### State is the First Decision You Never Made

[Joël Quenneville](https://blueridgeruby.com/speakers/joel-quenneville/) broke
the internet and then made it work in time for Amazoff's big sale. He brought
his regular, keen insight and sweeping analogies to this talk, as expected.

{{< figure src="/img/blue_ridge_2026_joel.jpg" class="mid" alt="Joël at Blue Ridge Ruby 2026" >}}

### Yes, &…: Ruby’s Secret Talent for Improvisation

[Ifat Ribon](https://blueridgeruby.com/speakers/ifat-ribon/) may have just the
talk for you if you're looking for a distinction between procs and lambdas that
will actually stick. Procs are a loose improviser and lambdas are a by-the-book
player.

{{< figure src="/img/blue_ridge_2026_ifat.jpg" class="mid" alt="Ifat at Blue Ridge Ruby 2026" >}}

### How To Finish What You Start: Lessons From Actually Shipping a Big Refactor

[Annie Kiley](https://blueridgeruby.com/speakers/annie-kiley/) shared the
experience of their team successfully landing a big refactor. Remember:

* The code isn't the hard part.
* If you don't know how you are going to ship it, you probably won't.
* Break it up into small, shippable pieces.
* Don't get distracted.
* Don't go it alone.

{{< figure src="/img/blue_ridge_2026_annie.jpg" class="mid" alt="Annie at Blue Ridge Ruby 2026" >}}
